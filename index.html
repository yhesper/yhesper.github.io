Hang "Hesper" Yin

I am a PhD student at UC San Diego Center for Visual Computing advised by professor Albert Chern.

My research interests are discrete differential geometry and geometric mechanics, as well as their very fun applications in computer graphics.

I received my bachelors degree with university honors at Carnegie Mellon University School of Computer Science, where I was fortunately a part of Keenan Crane’s Geometry Collective and Prof. David Lindlbauer’s Augmented Perception Lab. After that, I was very lucky to spend a wonderful summer interning at EPFL Geometric Computing Lab, and then a wonderful gap year working at Taichi Graphics.

During my PhD study, I also had amazing time interning at Pixar Animation Studios and Adobe Research.
